Lessons from 10 Years of Creative Direction

December 18, 2025 · 4 min read

A decade ago, I was convinced that great creative work was all about the idea. The breakthrough concept, the clever twist, the memorable visual. And while those things matter, ten years of leading creative teams has taught me that sustainable creative excellence is built on something else entirely.

Process Over Inspiration

Early in my career, I waited for inspiration to strike. Now I know that inspiration is the reward for showing up consistently with a solid process. The best work comes from disciplined exploration, not lightning-bolt moments.

We've built systems at Mighty Stone that create space for creativity while maintaining momentum. Regular check-ins, structured feedback loops, and clear decision frameworks. It sounds unglamorous, but it's what allows us to consistently deliver work we're proud of.

Listening Is a Creative Skill

I used to think the creative director's job was to have all the answers. Now I know it's to ask the right questions and really listen to the responses.

The best ideas often come from unexpected sources — a junior designer's offhand comment, a client's frustration about something unrelated, a conversation overheard in a coffee shop. But you only catch these if you're truly listening.

Constraints Fuel Creativity

Give someone unlimited resources and total freedom, and they'll often freeze. Give them smart constraints — a tight budget, a specific challenge, a clear focus — and watch them thrive.

Some of our best work at Mighty Stone has come from projects with the tightest constraints. Limitations force you to think differently, to find elegant solutions, to be more creative with less.

Relationships Matter More Than Portfolio

Your portfolio opens doors. But what keeps you in the room — and brings clients back — is how you make people feel. Are you someone who listens? Who makes collaboration energizing rather than exhausting? Who delivers on promises?

The clients who've stayed with us for years aren't here because we're the most awarded agency. They're here because we've built trust, shown up consistently, and made the creative process something they look forward to rather than dread.

Keep Learning

The moment you think you've figured it out is the moment you start falling behind. The industry evolves, audiences change, new tools emerge. Stay curious. Stay humble. Keep learning.

After ten years, I still feel like I'm just getting started. And that's exactly how it should be.
